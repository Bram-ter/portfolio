Pinned.

Pinned is a project I made together with 2 other students that followed the same preliminary education as me. We all decided to make an app for a good friend of us (who also followed the same preliminary education but became an entrepreneur). He always wanted to go on a road trip through Europe, where Spain was his favorite country. He couldn’t do his road trip because of Covid-19, and that’s why we decided to design an app that would eventually help him get through Spain on a fun and clear way.

Narrowing down our chosen concept

Below you can see the first flow of our app. This was the flow for the app as an all in one road trip to Europe, we later decided to narrow our concept down to only Spain because it would get to complicated and to much work for the assignment given to us by our school.

This flow is what we eventually came up with, and what would be our final flow for the app.

Determining the main categories for the app

Moodboard and first lo-fi screens

After coming up with what would be the main categories for the app, we made a mood-board together to determine a certain style for the app.

We wanted to go for a modern style, with happy and colorful looking illustrations for our map.

We also did some first sketches of some screens that would be in the app. Because these were first sketches, we decided to use this as a way to also brainstorm our ideas and show it to each other to determine what we would use in the app.

Making of the logo and style

Because we were making a travel app, we wanted the style to be modern and playful looking. This style would also fit our type of illustrations and logo the most.

We have also made a design system. We did this to be able to see everything we came up with for the style in 1 clear document.

As you can see in the design system. We put in the logo, and all primary and secondary colors we associated with the app. We also put in all the possible button styles (width, height, color) and buttons states. For example, a button being hovered or a button being clicked. And lastly the font, and where and when we would use a certain size of it.

Mi-fi and Hi-fi

Eventually we worked out our first Mi-fi screens. Below you can see a couple of the Map screen (Left), the stats screen of the map (Middle) and the shop screen (Right).

Hi-fi prototype

After a couple of weeks we made our high fidelity prototype. With making the hi-fi prototype we finally could really see some colors in our design. That’s why this was a great moment to see if all the colors would line up together. And see which color was used correctly or if a color was used too frequently.

After showing the prototype with Simon he was really happy. He found the app to be really clear and helpful. He could really see him using it when he would eventually go on his trip to Spain. The only thing he noted was that he was missing something about the nightlife, cause that was something he was still really interested in.

To the right you can see a link to our clickable demo, made with protopie. This was also the prototype we tested with Simon.
